# 🎮 TGJ2O – Culminating Project  
## 🧠 Class 1: Developing Your Game Idea

---

## 🎯 Goal for Today

Today is about **planning your board game concept**.

By the end of class, you should have:
- A clear **game idea (theme + concept)**
- A basic understanding of **how your game works**
- A completed **Notion entry with full planning details**

This is one of the most important days of the project.  
A strong idea here will make everything else easier.

---

## 💡 Keep It Simple

You are **not being graded on complexity**.

You are being evaluated on:
- 🎨 Visual design  
- 🧠 Planning and clarity  
- 🧩 How well your game actually functions  

A simple game that is **clear, playable, and well-designed** is far better than a complicated game that doesn’t work.

---

## 🤖 Using AI as a Tool (Not a Shortcut)

You will use AI (ChatGPT or similar) to help **develop your game idea**.

However:

- You are the **designer**
- AI is a **brainstorming tool**
- You must **guide, refine, and question** what it gives you  

### What Good AI Use Looks Like:
- Asking follow-up questions  
- Adjusting ideas to match your vision  
- Combining multiple suggestions  
- Reworking anything that doesn’t fit  

### What Not to Do:
- Copy and paste answers without thinking  
- Accept the first response without refinement  
- Let the AI fully decide your game  

📌 **You are responsible for the final idea.**

---

## 🧠 Step 1 – Brainstorm Your Game

Start by thinking about:

- 🎨 **Theme** (What is your world?)  
- 🎮 **Type of game** (Race, strategy, collection, etc.)  
- 🏆 **Objective** (What are players trying to do?)  
- 🔁 **Core gameplay loop** (What happens on a turn?)  

---

### 💬 Sample Prompt

Use this to get started:

> "I'm looking to create a simple board game. Please create a series of 5 questions to help develop a board game idea with rules. Keep it simple and appropriate for players aged 8+. Ask one question at a time and wait for my response before continuing."

⚠️ Do not stop after one response.  
Go back and forth until your idea is **clear and usable**.

---

## 🎨 Step 2 – Develop a Colour Palette

Your game should have a **consistent visual identity**.

Once your theme is decided:
- Ask AI for a **5-colour palette** that matches your theme
- Think about **mood, tone, and audience**

### Example Prompt:
> "My board game is based on a haunted mansion and should feel dark and mysterious but still fun. Suggest a 5-colour palette and explain what each colour represents."

---

### 🛠️ Refine Your Colours

Use: **https://coolors.co**

- Enter your colours  
- Lock ones you like  
- Adjust to create a clean, consistent palette  

📸 You will include a **screenshot of your palette** in your Notion.

---

## 📝 Step 3 – Complete Your Notion Entry

All of your work for today will be completed in your **Notion portfolio**.

Please complete the template for today. Your entry must include:

- Game name  
- Theme  
- Type of game (with explanation)  
- Objective and win condition  
- Basic rules / game flow  
- Game components (board, cards, pieces, etc.)  
- Colour palette (with hex codes + screenshot)  
- Link to your AI chat  

📌 This is not optional.  
This is how your **Thinking (planning)** is assessed.

---

## 🔍 Step 4 – Expectations

By the end of class:

- Your idea should be **clear and understandable**
- Your rules should be **simple but functional**
- Your Notion entry should be **complete and detailed**
- Your AI use should show **actual back-and-forth thinking**

If your idea is unclear or incomplete, it will slow you down in future classes.
