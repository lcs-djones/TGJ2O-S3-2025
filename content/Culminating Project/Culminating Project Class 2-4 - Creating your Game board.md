# 🎮 TGJ2O – Culminating Project  
## 🖼️ Classes 2–4: Designing Your Game Board

---

## 🎯 Goal for These Classes

You are now moving from **planning → production**.

By the end of these classes, you should have:
- A **fully designed game board in Photoshop**
- A **clear and functional layout that supports gameplay**
- A **detailed Notion entry showing your process and decisions**

Your board is the **centrepiece of your game**. Everything else connects to it.

![[Pasted image 20260325104351.png]]

---

## ⏱️ How to Use Your Time

- **Class 2:** Planning + sketch + Photoshop setup  
- **Class 3:** Building layout + core design  
- **Class 4:** Refinement + details + finalizing  

📌 Do not try to rush to “finish early.” Use the full time to improve quality.

---

## 🧠 Step 1 – Plan Your Board (Before Photoshop)

Do not jump straight into Photoshop.

Start by thinking about:

### 🎨 Theme & Visual Direction
- How does your board visually reflect your theme?
- Does it feel like the world your game takes place in?

### 🧩 Layout & Gameplay
- What type of layout fits your game?
  - Straight path  
  - Winding path  
  - Loop / circuit  
  - Grid  
  - Branching paths  

- How do players move?
- Where are key areas located (start, end, special zones, cards)?

📌 Your layout should support gameplay, not just look good.

![[Pasted image 20260325104436.png]]

---

## ✏️ Step 2 – Rough Sketch (Required)

Before designing digitally, you must create a **rough sketch on paper**.

Your sketch must include:
- Board shape (square or rectangle)
- Clear path or grid
- Start and End points
- At least 4 special squares
- Card areas (draw/discard)
- General layout of visuals and sections
- Placement ideas for shapes and title

📸 Take a photo of your sketch and include it in your Notion.

![[Pasted image 20260325104332.png]]

---

## 💻 Step 3 – Photoshop Setup

Set up your file correctly **before designing**:

- **Size:** 19.75” x 19.75”  
- **Resolution:** 300 PPI  
- **Colour Mode:** CMYK  
- **File Type:** PSD  

📌 If you ignore this, parts of your board may be cut off when printed.

![[Pasted image 20260325104959.png]]

---

## 🎨 Step 4 – Build Your Board

### 🌄 Background
- Must match your theme  
- Not just a plain colour  
- Should feel like part of the game world  

### 🧩 Board Layout
- Clear path or grid  
- Start and End clearly visible  
- Logical flow for gameplay  

### 🃏 Game Areas
- Card zones (draw/discard)  
- Special squares (minimum 4)  

---

## 🧱 Step 5 – Layer Organization (Required)

Your file must be **clean and organized**.

Use named layer groups:

- Background  
- Board Path / Spaces  
- Card Areas  
- Text & Labels  
- Decorative Elements  
- Special Squares  

❌ Do not leave layers named “Layer 1” or “Group 3”

📌 Organization is part of your mark.

![[Pasted image 20260325104507.png|350x400]]

---

## 🃏 Game Cards

Alongside your board, you are also responsible for designing your **game cards** if your game requires them.

Your cards should match your board’s:
- 🎨 Visual style  
- 🧩 Theme  
- 🔤 Fonts and colour palette  

Everything should feel like part of the **same game**.

![[Pasted image 20260403173636.png]]
---

## 🎯 Purpose

Your cards should:

- Support how your game is played  
- Clearly communicate actions or effects  
- Be easy to read and understand  
- Add to the overall experience of your game  

📌 If players don’t understand your cards, they won’t understand your game.

---

## 🃏 Card Design Requirements

Each card must include:

- A **clear title**  
- A **description or instruction**  
- A **visual element** (icon, image, or design)  
- A **consistent layout** across all cards  

📌 All cards should follow the same structure — do not make each one completely different.

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Inconsistent layouts between cards  
- Text that is too small or hard to read  
- Too much text on a single card  
- Cards that don’t clearly affect gameplay  
- Designs that don’t match your board  

---

## 🔍 Expectations by the End of Class 4

- Your board should be **complete or very close to complete**
- Your layout should be **clear and playable**
- Your cards are complete and **match the style of your game**
- Your design should match your **theme and colour palette**
- Your file should be **organized and structured properly**

---

- ✏️ Created and uploaded rough sketch  
- 💻 Set up Photoshop file correctly  
- 🎨 Designed a themed background  
- 🧩 Built a functional board layout  
- 🃏 Included card areas and special squares

---

## 🚫 Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Jumping straight into Photoshop without planning  
- Making something that “looks cool” but doesn’t function  
- Using a plain or lazy background  
- Poor layer organization  
- Rushing instead of refining
