# 🎮 TGJ2O – Culminating Project  
## 🧱 Classes 5–6: Designing Game Pieces (TinkerCad)

---

## 🎯 Goal for These Classes

You will design and create **3D-printed game pieces (tokens)** that match your board game.

By the end of these classes, you should have:
- Two **completed 3D models** in TinkerCad  
- Files ready for **3D printing (.STL)**  
- Designs that are **stable, functional, and connected to your theme**

![[Pasted image 20260403174440.png]]

---

## 🧩 What You Are Creating

You must create:

- **2 different game tokens**


📌 Your pieces should:
- Clearly connect to your game’s **theme**
- Be easy to **recognize and use**
- Be stable and able to **stand upright**

---

## 📏 Design Requirements

- **Base:** 30mm wide  
- **Height:** 40–50mm max  
- Must be able to **stand securely**  

📌 If your base is too small or uneven, your piece will not work properly.

![[Pasted image 20260403174511.png]]

---

## ✏️ Step 1 – Sketch Your Pieces (Required)

Before using TinkerCad, you must plan your designs.

Your sketches must include:
- Atleast two different token designs  
- Clear connection to your game’s theme  
- Labeled dimensions (base + height)  
- A stable base design  

---

### 💡 Design Tips

Think about how your piece will be printed:

- Avoid large overhangs or floating parts  
- Build from the **bottom up**  
- Keep shapes **simple and clean**  
- Complex designs can be split into parts and assembled later  

![[Pasted image 20260403174607.png|300x300]]
---

## 🤝 Step 2 – Peer Feedback (Required)

Before building digitally:

- Show your sketches to **at least 3 classmates**  
- Gather feedback using the **TAG method**  
- Make improvements to your design  

📌 Strong designs come from iteration, not first ideas.

---

## 💻 Step 3 – Build in TinkerCad

Create your models using **TinkerCad**:

- Create **2 separate designs** (one per token)  
- Start with correct dimensions  
- Use:
  - Basic shapes  
  - Grouping  
  - Alignment tools  

📌 Focus on clean construction and intentional design.

---

## 🔁 Step 4 – Refine Your Designs

- Get feedback on your digital models  
- Make adjustments and improvements  
- Ensure:
  - Stability  
  - Clarity of shape  
  - Connection to theme  

![[Pasted image 20260403174659.png|300x300]]
---

## 📤 Step 5 – Export for Printing

Export both models as **.STL files**

As a reminder on proper TinkerCad file submissions, refer to the [[Submitting your Tinkercad files| class website.]]

📌 These files will be used for printing.

---

## 📁 Final Expectations

By the end of these classes, you should have:

- Atleast two complete and refined token designs  
- STL files 
- Screenshots of your models  
- Evidence of sketching, feedback, and iteration in your Notion  

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Designs that are too thin or unstable  
- Ignoring size requirements  
- Overcomplicating shapes  
- Not thinking if your piece can stand  
- Skipping sketching or feedback

Your game pieces are part of how players interact with your game.

- Functional
