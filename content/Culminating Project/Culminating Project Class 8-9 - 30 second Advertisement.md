# 🎮 TGJ2O – Culminating Project  
## 🎬 Classes 8–9: 30-Second Commercial

---

## 🎯 Goal for These Classes

You will create a **30-second video commercial** that promotes your board game.

This is your chance to:
- 🎥 Showcase your game in a **creative and engaging way**
- 🎨 Communicate your game’s **theme, tone, and identity**
- 💻 Apply your **Premiere Pro editing skills**

---

## 💡 What This Is (and What It Isn’t)

This is **not** just a slideshow of your work.

This is a **commercial**.

Think:
- Social media ad  
- Trailer  
- Teaser  
- Promotional video  

It should feel like something someone would actually watch online.

---

## ⚠️ Important Constraint (and Opportunity)

You likely **do not have a fully completed physical game** to film.

That is expected.

Instead, you should creatively use:
- Screenshots of your board, cards, and designs  
- Screen recordings of your work in progress  
- Close-up shots of printed elements (if available)  
- Creative filming (hands, dice, reactions, etc.)  
- Text, music, and editing to build excitement  

📌 Strong commercials are about **presentation and storytelling**, not just what you physically have.

---

## 🎥 Creative Direction

Your commercial does **not** have to directly explain the game.

You can take different approaches:

### 🔹 Direct
- Show gameplay  
- Explain rules briefly  
- Highlight features  

### 🔹 Indirect (Teaser Style)
- Build mystery  
- Focus on theme or mood  
- Reveal elements slowly  
- Create curiosity instead of explaining everything  

📌 Many real commercials don’t fully explain the product — they make you interested.

---

## 🧠 Step 1 – Plan Your Video

Before opening Premiere Pro, plan:

- What is the **focus** of your commercial?
- What will the viewer **feel**? (fun, intense, spooky, competitive, etc.)
- What visuals will you include?
- What order will things appear in?

---

## ✏️ Step 2 – Rough Plan / Storyboard

Create a quick plan on paper or notes:

- What happens at the start?
- What happens in the middle?
- How does it end?

📸 Take a photo or screenshot and include it in your Notion.

---

## 💻 Step 3 – Premiere Pro Setup

- Create a new project  
- Organize your files (video, images, audio) before importing  
- Use a standard sequence (1080p recommended)

📌 Keep your files organized to avoid “media offline” issues.

---

## 🎬 Step 4 – Build Your Commercial

Your video must include:

### ⏱️ Length
- ~30 seconds (25–35 seconds acceptable)

### 🎞️ Visuals
- Combination of:
  - Video clips  
  - Images / screenshots  
  - Text overlays  

### 🔊 Audio
- Music or sound that matches your theme  
- Balanced audio levels  

### ✂️ Editing Techniques
- Cuts and pacing that match the tone  
- Transitions used intentionally (not randomly)  
- Text used clearly and effectively  

---

## 🧱 Step 5 – Structure Your Video

A strong commercial typically includes:

- 🎬 **Hook (first 3–5 seconds)**  
  Grab attention immediately  

- 🎯 **Main Content**  
  Show key visuals, theme, or gameplay  

- 🏁 **Ending**  
  Game title + strong final moment  

---

## 📝 Step 6 – Notion Entry (Daily Requirement)

Your Notion post must include:

- Storyboard / plan  
- Screenshots of your timeline  
- Clips or assets used  
- Tools and techniques used in Premiere Pro  
- Explanation of creative choices  
- Challenges and how you solved them  

📌 This is how your **Communication and Application** are assessed.

---

## 🔍 Expectations by the End of Class 9

- Your commercial should be **fully complete**
- It should be **clear, engaging, and intentional**
- It should reflect your **game’s identity and tone**
- Editing should feel **clean and purposeful**

---

## ✅ Final Checklist

- 🎬 30-second commercial completed  
- 🎯 Clear theme and direction  
- 🎥 Strong opening (hook)  
- ✂️ Clean editing and pacing  
- 🔊 Audio included and balanced  
- 📝 Detailed Notion entries completed  

---

## 🚫 Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Making a slideshow instead of a commercial  
- No clear direction or message  
- Overusing random transitions  
- Poor pacing (too slow or too rushed)  
- No strong opening or ending  

---

## 🎯 Reminder

This is your **final opportunity to showcase your work**.

Even if your physical game is incomplete, your commercial should make it feel:
- Exciting  
- Polished  
- Worth playing  

Make people want to play your game.
