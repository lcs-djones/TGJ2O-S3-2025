# 🎮 TGJ2O – Culminating Project Rubric  
## Board Game Development (KTCA)

---

## 📝 Why the Notion Portfolio Matters So Much

A major part of this project’s assessment is based on your **daily Notion entries and reflection questions**.

This is intentional.

Your final products show the **end result**. Your Notion portfolio shows:
- how you planned  
- how you used class time  
- what tools and techniques you learned  
- how you solved problems  
- how your ideas improved over time  

📌 In this course, **learning = process + product**.  

Because of this, your Notion portfolio is weighed heavily.

---

## ⚖️ Overall Weighting

- **Notion Portfolio + Daily Reflection Questions + Final Products + In-class evidence:** 


### Final Products Include:
- 🎨 Game Board  
- 🃏 Game Cards  
- 🧱 3D Game Pieces  
- 🎬 30-Second Commercial

# 🧠 Knowledge (K)  
*Understanding of tools, techniques, and concepts*

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Level 4** | Strong understanding of tools and techniques across all apps. Explains how and why tools are used clearly in Notion. |
| **Level 3** | Good understanding of tools and techniques. Explains most processes clearly with minor gaps. |
| **Level 2** | Basic understanding. Some confusion or vague explanations. |
| **Level 1** | Limited understanding. Little to no explanation of tools or incorrect use. |

### ✅ Strong Evidence Example:
> “I used the align tool in TinkerCad to center my shape on the base so it would print evenly. Without this, it would be off balance.”

### ❌ Weak Evidence Example:
> “I used some tools to make my shape.”

---

# 🧠 Thinking (T)  
*Planning, decision-making, and problem solving*

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Level 4** | Clear, thoughtful planning and strong problem solving. Decisions are intentional and improve the project. |
| **Level 3** | Good planning and logical decisions. Some evidence of revision and improvement. |
| **Level 2** | Some planning, but ideas are underdeveloped or inconsistent. |
| **Level 1** | Little planning or reflection. Work appears rushed or random. |

### ✅ Strong Evidence Example:
> “I changed my board from a straight path to a loop because my game involves collecting items, and a loop allows players to keep playing instead of ending early.”

### ❌ Weak Evidence Example:
> “I changed my board because I didn’t like it.”

---

# 💬 Communication (C)  
*Clarity, organization, and effectiveness*

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Level 4** | Very clear, organized, and easy to understand. Strong visuals and explanations. |
| **Level 3** | Mostly clear and organized with minor issues. |
| **Level 2** | Somewhat unclear or inconsistent. |
| **Level 1** | Difficult to understand. Poor organization and unclear explanations. |

### ✅ Strong Evidence Example:
- Cards all use the same layout  
- Text is readable and consistent  
- Rules are easy to follow  
- Notion entries clearly explain thinking  

### ❌ Weak Evidence Example:
- Random fonts and colours  
- Text too small to read  
- No clear explanation of gameplay  
- Notion entries are vague or incomplete  

---

# 🛠️ Application (A)  
*Technical skill, execution, and quality*

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Level 4** | High-quality, polished, and functional work across all parts of the project. |
| **Level 3** | Complete and functional with minor issues. |
| **Level 2** | Partially complete or inconsistent quality. |
| **Level 1** | Incomplete or major technical issues. |

### ✅ Strong Evidence Example:
- Board is aligned, readable, and clean  
- 3D pieces are stable and printable  
- Commercial is well-paced and engaging  

### ❌ Weak Evidence Example:
- Board is cluttered or confusing  
- 3D pieces don’t stand or can’t print  
- Commercial is just a slideshow with no structure  

---

# 📓 Notion Portfolio Expectations

Your Notion portfolio must show:
- daily progress (not done all at once at the end)  
- clear explanations of tools and techniques  
- thoughtful answers to reflection questions  
- required screenshots and files  
- honest reflection on challenges and improvements  

---


## ✅ Example of a STRONG Notion Entry  
  
### 📝 Written Recap  
> “Today I created the main board path in Photoshop using shape layers. I duplicated a rounded rectangle shape and used the align and distribute tools to evenly space 24 tiles along a curved path. I also used guides (0.375” margin) to ensure everything stayed within the safe print area. For the background, I layered a gradient and texture to match my theme instead of using a flat colour.”  
  
### 💭 Reflection Answer  
> “One issue I ran into was that my path spacing looked uneven when curved. I fixed this by adjusting the anchor points and manually aligning key tiles instead of relying only on auto-distribute. This made the board more readable and consistent for gameplay.”  
  
---

## ❌ Example of a WEAK Notion Entry

### 📝 Written Recap
> “I worked on my board.”

### 💭 Reflection Answer

## 🔍 Key Differences

| Strong Entry | Weak Entry |
|--|--|
| Specific tools mentioned | No tools mentioned |
| Explains decisions | No explanation |
| Shows thinking | Very surface-level |
| Connects to gameplay | No connection |
| Detailed and clear | Vague and short |
